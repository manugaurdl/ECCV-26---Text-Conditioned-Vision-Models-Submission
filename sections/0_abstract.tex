

\begin{abstract}
Pretrained Vision Transformers exhibit a \textit{saliency bias}: their global representation collapse toward the most prominent objects in a scene, ignoring task-relevant details.
Existing approaches either produce rich but query-agnostic representations (e.g., DINOv2) or accept text queries but produce task-specific representations that lack the generality for downstream transfer (e.g., SAM3, GroundingDINO).
To address this, we introduce SteerViT, a method that equips any Vision Transformer with the ability to steer both its global and dense visual features with natural language.
Our approach dynamically routes attention to task-relevant visual concepts, enabling us to guide the semantics of the visual representation with text while preserving their downstream transfer.
We demonstrate that our steerable visual representations enable a wide range of applications that are difficult to address with prior state-of-the-art models, such as fine-grained multimodal retrieval and referential segmentation.
SteerViT also matches or outperforms state-of-the-art baselines on zero-shot anomaly detection and personalized object discrimination, generalizing to out-of-distribution domains without task-specific training.
  \keywords{Visual Representation \and Semantic Steering \and Text-Conditioning}
\end{abstract}
