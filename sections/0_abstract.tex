
\begin{abstract}
General purpose visual encoders have a \textit{saliency} bias, where they effectively encode the image 
% through salient visual concepts
through the loudest visual concepts in the scene.
To address this, we introduce SteerViT, a method that equips any Vision Transformer with the ability to steer 
% the semantics of
its features with text. 
Our approach dynamically routes attention to task-relevant 
% visual concepts 
objects,
achieving a Pareto improvement in text steerability while preserving the visual representation quality.
% (unlike SAM3/GroundingDino??).
Through extensive experiments, we show that SteerViT can guide the semantics of its visual features with  text.
It consistently outperforms query-agnostic ViT baselines across model families (DinoV2, SigLIP, MAE) on fine-grained dense prediction and retrieval tasks, exhibiting strong generalization to out-of-distribution domains.
